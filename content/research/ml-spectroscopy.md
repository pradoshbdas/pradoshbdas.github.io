<h2>Machine Learning for Stellar Spectroscopy</h2>
<p>Machine learning techniques are increasingly used to analyze large spectroscopic datasets and extract stellar parameters efficiently.</p>
<h3>Research Goals</h3>
<ul>
<li>Predict stellar temperature, gravity, and metallicity</li>
<li>Identify unusual stellar spectra</li>
<li>Automate spectral classification</li>
</ul>
<h3>Techniques Used</h3>
<ul>
<li>Neural networks</li>
<li>Random forests</li>
<li>Dimensionality reduction (PCA, t-SNE)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Applications</h3>
<p>Machine learning models can process millions of stellar spectra from modern surveys and identify patterns that may be difficult to detect with traditional methods.</p>
<h3>Future Work</h3>
<p>Developing interpretable machine learning models that link spectral features to physical stellar properties.</p>
