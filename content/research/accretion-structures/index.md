        <article id="research-content" class="card markdown-body research-article-card"><h2>Accretion Structures</h2>
<p>Accretion processes occur when matter falls onto compact astrophysical objects such as black holes, neutron stars, or young stars.</p>
<h3>Research Questions</h3>
<ul>
<li>How do accretion disks transport angular momentum?</li>
<li>What mechanisms trigger accretion outbursts?</li>
<li>How does magnetic activity affect disk evolution?</li>
</ul>
<h3>Physical Processes</h3>
<ul>
<li>Magnetohydrodynamics</li>
<li>Disk turbulence</li>
<li>Radiative transfer</li>
</ul>
<h3>Observational Signatures</h3>
<p>Accretion structures can be studied through:</p>
<ul>
<li>Spectral line profiles</li>
<li>Variability studies</li>
<li>Multi-wavelength observations</li>
</ul>
<h3>Future Work</h3>
<p>Developing models that connect theoretical simulations with observational data from modern telescopes.</p>
